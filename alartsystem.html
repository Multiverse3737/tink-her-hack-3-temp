Low-Level Stress (Mild and Manageable)

This is the everyday stress that comes from small challenges. You can manage it with quick and simple techniques.
-Take deep breathouts (4-7-8 breathing).
-Stretch or do a quick workout
-Listen to calming music or nature sounds.
-Drink water or herbal tea.
-Take mini breaks from work/study.
-Laugh (watch funny videos, talk to a fun friend).
-Reduce screen time for a few minutes.

Medium-Level Stress (Noticeable and Distracting)

This stress affects focus and emotions but is still controllable.
-Exercise
-Write down what’s stressing you and possible solutions.
-Meditate or practice mindfulness (focus on the present moment).
-Break tasks into smaller parts and use time management techniques.
-Listen to upbeat music to shift your mood.
-Vent to someone (friend, family, or mentor).
-Practice gratitude—list three things you’re grateful for.

High-Level Stress (Overwhelming and Intense)

This stress feels uncontrollable and may cause anxiety, irritation, or exhaustion.
-Pause and slow down – Take a deep breath before reacting.
-Prioritize self-care – Ensure you're eating, sleeping, and exercising properly.
-Progressive Muscle Relaxation – Tense and relax each muscle group.
-Seek social support – Talk to close friends, family, or a counselor.
-Disconnect from stressors – Take a break from studying or work.
-Avoid caffeine or energy drinks – They can increase anxiety.
-Engage in a hobby
-Consider professional help if stress is overwhelming for a long time.
